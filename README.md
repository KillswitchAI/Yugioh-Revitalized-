Revitalized Yu-Gi-Oh! Format — Official Rules

Why This Format Exists
Modern Yu-Gi-Oh! abandoned its original design ecology in favor of commercial power creep, pricing out players and obsoleting cards arbitrarily. This format restores the original intent — a stable, honest game where every card has a dignified place, accessible to any player regardless of economic situation, with repeated value that never expires.

Core Philosophy
Every card earns its place through honest evaluation. Power is earned through trade-offs. Normal Monsters are the backbone and soul of the game. Artwork and flavor text are not decoration — they are the game’s humanity.

Foundational Rule — Combat Zone Classification

A card’s functional power level is determined by its combat zone, not its printed level. Combat zones are defined by dual ATK/DEF thresholds and layered with tribute cost to produce an accurate power assessment.

Combat Zone Matrix



|Zone  |ATK Range|DEF Range|
|------|---------|---------|
|Zone 1|0–1000   |0–500    |
|Zone 2|1001–1500|501–1200 |
|Zone 3|1501–1900|1201–1800|
|Zone 4|1901–2400|1801–3000|

Zone Commitment Modifier (Level 1–4)

	•	True Specialist — stats diverge significantly across zones. Standard zone cost.
	•	Partial Specialist — one stat clearly zoned higher, other slightly lower. −3 to −5 points.
	•	Non-Specialist — both stats same zone, no commitment. −6 to −10 points.
	•	Extreme Non-Specialist — both stats low, same zone. −11 to −15 points.
	•	Edge Case Specialty — extreme divergence, clear design identity. +3 to +8 points.

Above Class Premium
Cards that perform significantly above their level class in combat prowess are priced above their level ceiling but below the next level’s floor. Giant Soldier of Stone is the benchmark — the acceptable ceiling for Level 3 combat prowess. Cards matching or exceeding this benchmark get flagged for premium pricing or format review.

Tribute Layer

	•	Level 1–4: no modifier. Base zone cost only.
	•	Level 5–6: +10 points. One tribute required.
	•	Level 7+: +20 points. Two tributes required.

Functional Identity Rule
A card’s functional classification is determined by how it performs in its combat zone, not its printed card type or level. If a card functions as a Normal Monster in a duel — no effects, stats only — it is treated as one for format purposes. Players declare functional role at deployment.

Tribute Monster Standard
Every tribute monster must justify its resource cost through combat zone performance. Cards that do not deliver Zone 3 or 4 performance for their tribute cost do not belong in the format as tribute monsters.

Fundamental Rules

	•	Effect Monster Limit: Maximum 7 Effect Monsters per deck. Ritual and Fusion monsters do not count.
	•	Level 4 Cap: Maximum 5 Level 4 monsters per deck total, across all monster types. Evaluated by printed level. Functional reclassification does not affect cap counting.
	•	Ritual & Fusion Limits: Maximum 1 Ritual Monster and 2 Fusion Monsters per deck.
	•	Deck Size: 40–50 cards.
	•	Target Split: 20 monsters / 20 spells and traps.

Dupe Power Tax — Deck Power Punishments
Duplicates are deck power punishments. Running more than one copy increases Power Point cost compounding at +50% per extra copy, or +25% for Fusions. The more impactful the card the harsher the punishment for duplicating it.

Power Points System

Every card has a base Power Point value derived from its combat zone, tribute cost, and zone commitment modifier.
Total Deck Value Limit: 1100 points flat regardless of deck size.
Fusion Perk: Normal Fusion Monsters are free at 0 base cost. Non-Normal Fusions have 30% lower base cost than comparable Effect Monsters. All Fusions use +25% compounding on duplicates.

Effect Monster Pricing
Effect Monsters are evaluated on two axes:

	•	Combat zone performance in the original card ecosystem baseline
	•	Effect utility tier (low / medium / high impact)

Double checkbox cards — meaningful combat zone AND strong effect utility — carry a premium reflecting both dimensions. Single checkbox cards — strong effect OR combat zone but not both — priced accordingly on their dominant axis.

Tier System (Soft Guideline, Linked to Points)

	•	Tier 0 — ≤30 points. Humble cards. Strongly encouraged.
	•	Tier 1 — 31–70 points. Balanced cards.
	•	Tier 2 — 71–120 points. High impact cards.
	•	Tier 3 — 121+ points. Format-warping. Heavy punishment for duplicates.

Hardcore Rules (Optional)

	•	Effects 1–5: normal base cost.
	•	Effect 6: base cost ×1.5.
	•	Effect 7: base cost ×2.

Reprint Set Design Principles

Cards eligible for the curated reprint set must satisfy:

	•	Honest combat zone placement with no artificial inflation
	•	Tribute cost justified by zone performance
	•	Artwork with genuine character
	•	Flavor text with real voice
	•	Numbers calibrated to fill genuine gaps in zone distribution
	•	Edge case and above class cards evaluated individually

New card designs may be commissioned to fill persistent zone gaps. Stat adjustments follow distribution logic not arbitrary inflation.

All values are prototype starting points subject to playtesting and community consensus.
